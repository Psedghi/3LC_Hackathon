3LC Data-Centric AI Hackathon: Strategy & Execution

This weekend, my main strategy for the Chihuahua vs. Muffin challenge was to ruthlessly optimize the dataset rather than the model architecture, strictly adhering to the fixed ResNet-18 constraint. We started by utilizing the 3LC Dashboard to generate 3D UMAP embeddings of the 3,579 unlabeled images. Instead of randomly labeling, we targeted high-ROI clusters—specifically boundary cases where the model's confidence was hovering around 0.5. By iteratively shifting these "hard examples" into our active training set, we built a perfectly balanced dataset of over 400 chihuahuas and 400 muffins.

Once we hit the 85% accuracy wall, we transitioned from bulk labeling to precision margin optimization. We audited our labeled training data by sorting for high-loss samples to eliminate human error and correct mislabels. More importantly, we applied an aggressive weighting strategy using 3LC, boosting the sample weights of our lowest-confidence edge cases to 1.5. This mathematically forced the network to pay significantly more attention to its visual blind spots during training without overfitting on the easy, standard classifications.

For the final push to break the 90%+ threshold, we polished the engineering pipeline while remaining 100% rule-compliant. We introduced Label Smoothing, and heavy data augmentations (like  RandomRotation) to make the model robust against visual noise. Finally, we deployed Test-Time Augmentation (TTA) in our prediction script, averaging the logits of multiple augmented views for each hidden test image to maximize our confidence and secure our final Kaggle leaderboard score.
